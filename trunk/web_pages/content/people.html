People

So far only a couple people are involved, since the project is just getting started. We hope to gradually add more names to the list.

Brian Douglas Skinner
I wrote my first computer program a quarter century ago, on a Teletype machine that had no screen and that saved files by punching holes in a paper tape. Over the years things have improved a lot, as computers gained disk drives, and screens, and faster processors. In recent years there's been more progress still, with the creation of the internet and the world wide web, which I consider one of the great human achievements, right up there with things like penicillin, and democracy, and Gutenberg's press. I'm excited about each new advance, and hope to play a small role in helping to move us forward. Currently, I'm happily unemployed, living in San Francisco and working on the OpenRecord project.
Chih-Chao Lam
Having started two venture-backed companies, ShoppingList and ClickOver, Chao is a veteran, beneficiary and casualty of the Internet boom. Chao is curious about why certain innovations catch on and others die by the wayside. Chao and Brian first met while working at OSAF, and Chao is contributing to the OpenRecord project in his spare time while he explores ideas for his next start-up.
Mignon Belongie
Mignon is the most recent programmer to join the OpenRecord project. Mignon went to college at Caltech and did her doctoral work at Cornell, and then went on to do rocket science at NASA's JPL. When she's not working on OpenRecord, Mignon holds down a day job at DigitalPersona in Redwood City.
